Ah, "Kind of Blue," the album that every self-proclaimed jazz aficionado clutches to their chest like a security blanket. Let's be real, this is the musical equivalent of beige. It's so inoffensive and safe, it's practically elevator music for intellectuals. You know you're dealing with an overrated snoozefest when people praise it for being "atmospheric." Translation: it's background music for pretentious dinner parties where everyone pretends to understand wine. Miles Davis? More like Miles Mediocre on this one. He just had the luck of assembling some other extremely talented musicians with great chemistry and his reputation came from it. Face it, some of his other work is way better, but you'll never admit it because you're too busy trying to impress your friends with your "refined" taste. It is not even that good. The high rating is because of its perceived quality rather than its real quality.
